About Spanish Fork

A Growing City with
Small-Town Heart

Spanish Fork sits at the southern end of Utah Valley, nestled between the Wasatch Mountains and Utah Lake. It's one of Utah County's fastest-growing cities — but it's managed to hold onto that friendly, welcoming community feel that makes people want to stay.

Whether you're drawn by affordable homes, excellent schools, quick freeway access to Provo and Salt Lake, or the incredible outdoor recreation right outside your door — Spanish Fork has a lot to offer families of every size.

Outdoor Activities & Recreation
Parks, trails, and adventure right outside your door
Hobble Creek Canyon
Hiking · Camping
One of Utah County's most beloved canyons, just minutes east of town. Hiking trails, fishing, picnic spots, and campgrounds make this a year-round favorite for families.
Drive from SF: 10–15 min · Free access · Dog-friendly
Spanish Fork River Trail
Free · Paved
A paved multi-use trail along the Spanish Fork River — perfect for walking, biking, and running. Connects to the broader Utah Valley trail network.
Great for: Kids, strollers, cyclists · Free · Year-round
Spanish Fork Hot Pot
Hidden Gem
A unique geothermal hot spring along the river. A quirky local secret — not fancy, but totally memorable. Worth the short hike to get there.
Location: Near Spanish Fork River · Free · Seasonal
Payson Lakes
Fishing · Hiking
About 45 minutes up Payson Canyon — stunning alpine lakes perfect for trout fishing, short hikes, and summer picnics. A local favorite for families.
Drive: 45 min · Day-use fee · Peak season: June–Sept
Ski Utah — Sundance & Deer Valley
Winter · Skiing
World-class skiing within an hour's drive. Sundance Resort (Robert Redford's place) is 35 minutes from Spanish Fork — uncrowded, beautiful, and affordable.
Sundance: 35 min · Park City: ~75 min
Nebo Scenic Loop
Scenic Drive · Hiking
One of Utah's most beautiful scenic byways, ringing Mount Nebo — Utah's tallest Wasatch peak. Stunning fall color drives, wildflower hikes, and overlooks.
Drive: 20 min to start · Free · Best: June–Oct

About Mapleton

Peaceful, Spacious &
Close to Everything

Mapleton is where I chose to raise my family — and I'll tell you exactly why: it feels like the best of both worlds. Quiet, tree-lined streets with larger lots and a true neighborhood feel, but you're 5 minutes from Spanish Fork and 20 minutes from Provo.

Mapleton has a strong sense of community, great schools (Nebo School District), and jaw-dropping mountain views from almost every street. Homes here tend to have more land, more privacy, and more elbow room than anywhere else in the valley.

"This is where I live. This is where I raised my kids. If you're considering Mapleton — call me. I'll walk you through every neighborhood personally."
— Tammy Seiler, Mapleton Resident

About Springville

Utah's "Art City" —
Culture, Community & Charm

Springville is known throughout Utah as the "Art City" — and for good reason. Home to one of the oldest art museums in the state and a deep tradition of creativity, Springville has a character all its own. It's friendly, affordable, and quietly one of the best-kept secrets in Utah County.

Close to Provo, BYU, and all of Utah County's major employers, Springville offers excellent value for homebuyers — great schools, strong community identity, and quick access to everything you need.

Springville's creative spirit sets it apart

Must Visit · Free
One of the finest art museums in Utah — and it's free! Rotating exhibitions of American art in a beautiful historic building. A genuine cultural gem that most people outside Utah don't know about.
Location: 126 E 400 S · Springville · Free admission · Tue–Sat
Hobble Creek Golf Course
Golf · Scenic
A beautiful 18-hole public golf course at the mouth of Hobble Creek Canyon. Stunning mountain views, well-maintained greens, and affordable rates make this a local favorite.
Address: Springville · Public course · Season: April–Oct
